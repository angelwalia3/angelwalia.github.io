HapTech

A Gaming Console for the Visually Impaired

Selected as a finalist at CHI Student Game Competition 2020

Motivation
Critical components of todayâ€™s video games are their ability to provide life-like visuals. However, graphics prove to be ineffective when we delve into developing games for the visually impaired. In this work, I have explored how haptics and tactile interfaces can be harnessed to provide the visually impaired a better gaming experience.

Contextual Inquiry
My team and I interviewed nine visually impaired students aged 15-18 years to understand how they consumed games. We reached out to students from our institute and the National Association of Blind, Delhi.

Prototype Evolution
We conceptualised the HapTech console by dwelling on the inferences from the contextual inquiry. We started with cardboard prototypes. We followed a participatory design approach to evolve our console design.

Our objective was to come up with an ergnomic design for the console. After the cardboard prototyping phase, we 3-D printed the shell and decided and iterated upon the grid layout.

Final Solution
The equipment consisted of 3 main componenets - The console harware, the app, and the game to be played using the above.

The console consists of a 7x4 matrix made using vibration motors. This matrix acts as the user interface upon which the gameplay is projected for the haptic interaction. Additionally, there is an LED grid that gives a visual representation of the vibration matrix. It allows other people to witness the action that is taking place on the vibration matrix.

The app connects to the gaming console via Bluetooth. It consists of a top pane with a button for establishing connection and displaying the score. The bottom part of the screen is the controller for moving the game object using swipes in any direction. The app is also responsible for audio feedback to the user.

The game we programmed and evaluated was The Egg Catcher Game. It is an arcade-style game where in eggs drop from the sky, and the user controls a basket to catch the falling eggs. The user can move the basket left or right.

Evaluating HapTech
Final evaluation of the equipment was done with the help of studens from National Association for Blind, Delhi.
